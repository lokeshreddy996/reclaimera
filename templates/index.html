About Reclaim Era

Reclaim Era is the environmental sustainability initiative of Lamp Educational and Charitable Trust (LEACT). Founded on March 26, 2019, from a humble home in R.K. Hegde Nagar and now operating from our dedicated hub in Kogilu, we bridge the gap between ecological neglect and social inequality in Bengaluru.

🌍 Our Vision

To build a sustainable world where everyday waste is transformed into wisdom—giving discarded items a second life to support those in need, while empowering the next generation with the education to protect our environment.

🎯 Our Mission

To transform Bengaluru's everyday waste into wisdom by reclaiming textiles and household items to fund education, create green livelihoods, and protect our environment.

A Dual Crisis: Textile Waste & Theoretical Education

Bengaluru processes only a fraction of its 6,500 tonnes of daily waste. We are stepping in to solve two critical, unaddressed gaps:

🧺 The "Invisible" Textile Burden

Bengaluru generates 220 tonnes of textile waste daily. Lacking collection infrastructure, over 75% is illegally burned, releasing toxins that spike the Air Quality Index (AQI) and forcing sanitary workers to handle hazardous mixed waste.

📚 The Educational Disconnect

While NEP 2020 mandates environmental education, schools struggle. Students memorize textbook concepts without the campus infrastructure to practice waste segregation, and overburdened teachers lack the time to design hands-on sustainability programs.

Our Decentralized Circular Model

We execute our core protocol to support Bengaluru's vision of a landfill-free city.

♻️ Revive (Community Relief)

We collect and sanitize good-quality, wearable clothes and household items, distributing them directly to rural communities and those in need.

🏭 Recycle (Zero Landfill)

Torn or soiled fabrics are channeled to industrial shredders, strictly diverting them from landfills and toxic open burning.

📈 Rise (Fund & Empower)

We upcycle heavy fabrics like denim into premium lifestyle products. 100% of the proceeds from these products directly fund LEACT's educational programs and teacher salaries.

How We Work: The Journey from Waste to Wisdom

1

Hyper-Local Collection

We gather discarded textiles and household items through community drop-offs, corporate drives, and campus revive stations.

2

Sorting & Triage

Once the waste arrives at our Kogilu hub, our trained team weighs, sorts, and categorizes every item based on our 3-Way Recovery Protocol.

3

Revive, Recycle, Rise

Good items are sanitized and donated; torn fabrics are recycled; durable fabrics are upcycled into premium products.

4

Delivering the Wisdom

100% of proceeds from upcycled products fund LEACT's educational programs, teacher salaries, and classroom infrastructure.
