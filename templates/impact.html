A Year of Action: Turning Waste into Wisdom

We don't just talk about sustainability. We measure our impact in tonnes diverted, livelihoods created, and students educated.

The Crisis We Are Solving

Bengaluru is facing a silent environmental emergency. While the city processes some of its 6,500 tonnes of daily waste, two critical gaps remain completely unaddressed:

🧺 The "Invisible" Textile Burden

Bengaluru generates 220 tonnes of textile waste daily. Lacking systematic collection infrastructure, over 75% is illegally burned. This releases micro-plastics and toxins that spike the Air Quality Index (AQI) above 120, while forcing sanitary workers to manually handle hazardous mixed waste.

📚 The Educational Disconnect

This civic failure stems directly from our education system. While NEP 2020 mandates environmental education, schools struggle to implement it practically. Students memorize textbook concepts like climate change, but lack the campus infrastructure to actually practice segregation. Meanwhile, overburdened educators lack the time and specialized training to design hands-on sustainability programs.

The Result: We are raising a generation aware of global climate change, but completely unequipped to manage the waste generated in their own neighbourhoods.

The Solution: A Decentralized "Education-First" Circular Model

Reclaim Era proposes a dual-impact model that combines practical waste infrastructure with behavioral education. By addressing both the physical waste and the civic mindset, we support Bengaluru's vision of a landfill-free city.

♻️ Strategic Textile Recovery

Through our hyper-local processing hub in Kogilu, we execute our core protocol: Revive (donate wearables), Recycle (shred unusable fabrics), and Rise (upcycle heavy fabrics into premium products to fund operations).

📚 Educational Integration

We bridge the gap between theoretical science and practical civic action by conducting "Zero Waste Audits" for institutions and implementing hands-on workshops to help schools meet their NEP 2020 mandates.

👩🏽 Livelihood Generation

We transform waste management from a basic "cleaning" task into a skilled, dignified livelihood by training women from low-income groups in tailoring and upcycling.

The Proof (The Numbers)

1. The "Revive" Pillar: Community Relief

11,000+ Items Diverted & Donated: We have successfully processed and distributed over 11,000 used garments, toys, books, and household essentials.

Direct Beneficiaries: These resources were channeled directly to Below Poverty Line (BPL) families, migrant laborers, and vulnerable street and transit communities across the city.

2. The "Rise" Pillar: Green Livelihoods & Education

Women's Economic Empowerment: We successfully transitioned waste management into skilled labor by creating consistent, green livelihoods for 6 women. Since November 2025, these artisans have been earning a stable income of ₹8,000 per month through upcycling initiatives.

Sustaining Core Education: The revenue generated through our circular model has successfully funded the core operational costs of LEACT, directly paying for after-school teacher salaries, facility rent, and essential utilities.

3. The "Wisdom" Pillar: Civic Awareness

🎓 Grassroots Workshops

In October 2025, we launched our hands-on Waste Management awareness workshops, actively training students across four Government Schools (Thanisandra, Kempapura, Devanahalli, and Vijayapura).

📝 The Next Frontier

June 2026: We are finalizing a historic Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) to officially integrate a comprehensive, 1-year Environmental Education curriculum for 7th to 10th-grade students.

🏆 NEP 2020 Compliance

We formally bridge the gap between NEP 2020 mandates and practical civic action, helping schools meet their environmental education requirements.
