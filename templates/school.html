Bridging the Gap Between Theory and Action

Under the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020, practical environmental education is a core mandate. However, schools face a significant challenge: how do you teach sustainability effectively when teachers are already overburdened with core academics?

📖 Theory Over Action

Teaching climate change from a textbook fails to create lasting behavioral change. Furthermore, a disconnect occurs when students learn about recycling in the classroom but do not have the infrastructure to practice it on campus.

👨‍🏫 Overburdened Teachers

Educators lack the time and specialized training to design hands-on sustainability programs, turning environmental science into just another exam subject rather than a civic lifestyle.

Reclaim Era provides the complete solution. We bring the infrastructure and the curriculum directly to your school.

The 1-Year "Waste to Wisdom" Curriculum

We partner with educational institutions to provide a comprehensive, hands-on environmental program tailored specifically for 7th to 10th-grade students. We turn your campus into a living laboratory for the circular economy.

🎓 Expert-Led Interactive Workshops

Our specialized educators conduct dynamic sessions on waste segregation, the lifecycle of textiles, the micro-plastics crisis, and the mechanics of a circular economy.

🔍 Student-Led Campus Audits

We move beyond lectures. We train your students to conduct actual "Zero Waste Audits" of their own school, teaching them critical analytical and problem-solving skills.

♻️ The Campus "Revive Station"

We install a permanent, branded collection bin on your campus. This allows students to practice what they learn immediately by safely donating old clothes, books, and e-waste from their homes.

🏆 Impact Reporting & Certification

At the end of the academic year, students who complete the program receive a "Green Ambassador Certificate." The school receives a detailed Impact Report showing exactly how many kilograms of waste the student body successfully diverted from landfills.
